Here's an elaborate recipe for Bacon-Wrapped Dates: Ingredients: 24 Medjool dates, pitted 12 slices bacon, cut in half 24 whole almonds (optional, for stuffing) 1/4 cup balsamic glaze (optional, for drizzling) Toothpicks or skewers (if needed) Instructions: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. If desired, stuff each pitted date with a whole almond. Simply make a small incision in each date and insert the almond. Skip this step if you prefer plain dates. Wrap each date with a half slice of bacon, ensuring it covers the entire date. Secure the bacon in place by using toothpicks or skewers if needed. Place the bacon-wrapped dates on the prepared baking sheet, seam side down, with enough space between each date for even cooking. Bake the bacon-wrapped dates in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the bacon is crispy and golden brown. Remove the baking sheet from the oven and transfer the bacon-wrapped dates to a serving platter. If desired, drizzle the bacon-wrapped dates with balsamic glaze for added flavor and presentation. Serve the bacon-wrapped dates warm as an appetizer or snack. Tips: For a variation, you can stuff the dates with other ingredients such as goat cheese, blue cheese, or cream cheese, instead of almonds. If you prefer a sweeter version, sprinkle the bacon-wrapped dates with a bit of brown sugar before baking. If you don't have toothpicks or skewers, you can secure the bacon by placing the seam side down on the baking sheet. Bacon-wrapped dates make a delightful combination of sweet and savory flavors. They are perfect for parties or as an elegant appetizer. Enjoy!
